# Dholakia Retail Corporate Website — High-Fidelity UI Blueprint & Detailed Design Prompts

This document defines the final high-fidelity UI direction for the Dholakia Retail corporate website and is written from the combined perspective of a Senior UI/UX Designer, Marketing Researcher, and CEO. The experience is designed to position Dholakia Retail as a premium luxury retail group with strong heritage, clear portfolio architecture, corporate credibility, and future-ready digital sophistication. The direction below is based on the attached strategic material, which frames Dholakia Retail as a luxury parent brand that must balance emotional storytelling, portfolio navigation, ESG trust, investor clarity, and partner conversion while drawing inspiration from luxury conglomerates such as LVMH, Richemont, and Kering. [1][2]

## Strategic Design Positioning

The website should not feel like a standard corporate brochure. It must behave like a luxury flagship portal where brand image, institutional trust, and portfolio discoverability work together. The experience should communicate authority in the first screen, then reveal depth in measured layers: group identity, craftsmanship, manufacturing excellence, portfolio brands, sustainability, investor confidence, talent, and contact. The site must support multiple user intents, including luxury brand exploration, business partnership research, investor due diligence, media reference, and careers discovery. [1][2]

The visual direction should follow a modern noir language with cinematic restraint: deep charcoal and black surfaces, ivory or warm off-white typography, heritage-gold accents, elevated photography, and editorial spacing. Typography should combine a refined high-contrast serif for display moments with a clean sans-serif for body content and utility UI. Motion should feel slow, elegant, and deliberate rather than flashy. Every page should privilege clarity over decoration, and every CTA should feel intentional. [1][2]

## Final Page List

The minimum core website should include the following pages, all of which are justified by the strategic reports and user journey needs: Homepage, About Us, Leadership & Governance, Manufacturing Excellence, Our Portfolio (Brands Hub), Single Brand Page template, Sustainability & CSR (ESG), Investor Relations Portal showcase page, News & Press listing, Single News Page, Talent & Culture (Careers), Contact Us, and key utility pages such as Privacy Policy, Terms, 404, and Reports/Downloads where needed. The source material also supports a strong utility layer for investor access, press access, and brand pathways. [1][2]

## Design System Prompt — 1000+ Words

Create a world-class high-fidelity design system for **Dholakia Retail Private Limited**, a luxury retail parent company in the jewelry and premium lifestyle space. The website must feel like the digital flagship of a global luxury conglomerate rather than a typical Indian corporate site. The system should synthesize the emotional elegance of luxury brand storytelling with the structural confidence of investor-grade corporate communication. The visual tone is **Modern Noir Luxury**: dark, cinematic, editorial, refined, premium, architectural, and restrained. It should feel as if LVMH, Richemont, and Kering were reinterpreted through an Indian luxury retail lens rooted in heritage, innovation, craftsmanship, and corporate scale. [1][2]

Use a dark-first visual language built around layered tones such as rich black, deep charcoal, graphite, smoked bronze, and soft champagne. The primary background should feel like velvet-black or polished onyx. Secondary surfaces should create subtle elevation rather than obvious contrast. Text should use warm ivory or muted pearl white rather than pure white wherever possible. Accent color should be heritage gold or champagne-gold, used sparingly for CTAs, dividers, micro-highlights, navigation states, and key emphasis. Sustainability sections may introduce a controlled deep emerald accent, while investor or dashboard surfaces can introduce graphite blue-gray as a utility layer, but the design system must remain primarily monochrome and gold-led. Avoid colorful corporate gradients, generic SaaS blue-purple aesthetics, or loud luxury clichés. [1]

Typography must communicate prestige and clarity at once. Use a high-contrast serif for hero headlines, section titles, pull quotes, and important editorial moments. This serif should feel bespoke, elegant, and timeless. Pair it with a clean geometric or neo-grotesque sans-serif for navigation, paragraphs, metadata, forms, dashboards, labels, and buttons. The system should establish a clear scale: a cinematic hero display size; section heading scale; card-title scale; utility heading scale; body-large; body-standard; caption; and data label sizes. Letter spacing should be carefully adjusted, especially in uppercase micro-labels. Headlines should feel calm, not oversized for the sake of drama. Body text should be highly readable even on dark surfaces. Investor content must never sacrifice legibility for style. [1][2]

The layout system should use a generous 12-column desktop grid with strong asymmetry where useful, but overall discipline. Negative space is a design asset and should be used to create luxury pacing. The homepage and editorial sections can breathe, while data-rich pages such as Investor Relations and News archives should become more structured and modular. All layouts should adapt responsively across desktop, tablet, and mobile. On mobile, the experience should remain premium and simplified rather than merely compressed. Visual hierarchy must be maintained by reflowing modules carefully, not shrinking everything uniformly. [1][2]

Build a component system that includes a transparent-to-solid sticky header, utility header for investor/press/contact shortcuts, mega-menu for portfolio navigation, full-screen hero modules, editorial split-content sections, cinematic quote blocks, brand cards, KPI/stat modules, ESG impact counters, report-download cards, investor data cards, article cards, team profiles, timeline modules, accordion content blocks, comparison tables, forms, CTA banners, and a highly structured footer. Each component should have defined desktop, tablet, and mobile states and clear hover, active, focus, and disabled interactions. Hover interactions should be subtle: image zoom on cards, underline drift on text links, gold highlight glow on buttons, and slow opacity transitions. [1][2]

The motion system should feel premium and cinematic. Use slow fade-ups, image mask reveals, controlled parallax, and elegant staggered entrances. Avoid bouncy microinteractions, gimmicky 3D effects, and fast UI animations. Hero media should feel immersive but never heavy-handed. Scroll-triggered motion should reveal craftsmanship, scale, and impact as users progress through the site. KPI counters in ESG or investor modules can animate, but the movement must feel measured and credible. Navigation transitions should reinforce continuity. [1]

Imagery art direction is essential. Use macro shots of diamonds, artisan hands, luxury materials, elegant manufacturing scenes, executive portraiture with dramatic lighting, and editorial brand photography. Imagery must feel expensive, not stock-like. All visuals should support one of four narratives: heritage, craftsmanship, portfolio excellence, or trust. Brand photography in the portfolio should differentiate each subsidiary while preserving umbrella-brand sophistication. Sustainability visuals should feature real-world community and environmental impact without becoming charity-styled or overly emotional. Investor visuals should shift toward structured dashboards, documents, and data-backed corporate confidence. [1][2]

The design language must support multiple user types. For luxury consumers, it must inspire desire and curiosity. For partners, it must communicate scale and business credibility. For investors, it must provide frictionless access to proof, reports, governance, and ESG data. For media, it must be reference-friendly. For talent, it must feel aspirational and culturally strong. This means the system needs both emotional and functional modes. Emotional pages can be immersive and editorial; functional pages must become clean, minimal, and efficient while still staying on-brand. [1]

The dashboard-facing UI within Investor Relations should look like a luxury executive interface rather than a SaaS admin template. Use dark graphite surfaces, thin gold separators, subtle cards, refined charts, and typography that prioritizes legibility. Cards should contain revenue, growth, employee count, sustainability metrics, region or export overview, reports, and recent disclosures. Use minimalist sparkline-style charts, tabular figures, and download tables with clear hierarchy. The dashboard should feel boardroom-grade, not retail-ecommerce. [1]

Accessibility, performance, and credibility are non-negotiable. Ensure strong text contrast on dark backgrounds, visible focus states, accessible forms, readable tables, and mobile-safe tap targets. Avoid relying only on motion or color to communicate meaning. Prioritize fast-loading images, clear information architecture, and a polished sense of trust. The end result should be a premium corporate ecosystem that makes Dholakia Retail feel globally competitive, strategically mature, and visually unforgettable. [1][2]
